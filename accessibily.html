Accessibility at AIAutomatic

We're committed to creating digital experiences that are accessible to everyone, regardless of ability, disability, or assistive technology usage.

Our Commitment to Accessibility

At AIAutomatic, we believe that automation solutions should be accessible to everyone. We're dedicated to ensuring our website, products, and services meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 AA standards, providing an inclusive experience for all users.

We continuously work to identify and remove barriers that might prevent people with disabilities from accessing our content and using our services effectively.

Accessibility Features

We've implemented numerous features to ensure our platform is accessible to all users

Screen Reader Support

Full compatibility with popular screen readers including JAWS, NVDA, and VoiceOver with proper ARIA labels and semantic HTML.

Keyboard Navigation

Complete keyboard accessibility with logical tab order, visible focus indicators, and keyboard shortcuts for common actions.

High Contrast Mode

Support for high contrast modes and sufficient color contrast ratios (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text).

Resizable Text

Text can be resized up to 200% without loss of content or functionality, supporting users with low vision.

Alternative Text

All meaningful images have descriptive alternative text, and decorative images are marked as such for screen readers.

Reduced Motion

Respects users' motion preferences by disabling animations and transitions when reduced motion is preferred.

WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

Perceivable

  • Text alternatives for non-text content
  • Captions and other alternatives for multimedia
  • Create content that can be presented in different ways
  • Make it easier for users to see and hear content

Operable

  • Make all functionality available from a keyboard
  • Provide users enough time to read and use content
  • Do not use content that causes seizures
  • Provide ways to help users navigate and find content

Understandable

  • Make text content readable and understandable
  • Make content appear and operate in predictable ways
  • Help users avoid and correct mistakes

Robust

  • Maximize compatibility with current and future user agents
  • Assistive technology compatibility
